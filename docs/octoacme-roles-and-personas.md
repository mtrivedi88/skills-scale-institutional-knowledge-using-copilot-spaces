# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers collaborate with Product Managers and Developers to ensure features are intuitive, accessible, and meet real user needs. They translate requirements into user-centered designs and validate solutions before and after release.

### Responsibilities
- Lead user research and synthesize findings for the team
- Produce wireframes, prototypes, and usability test plans
- Review deliverables for UX alignment prior to release
- Advocate for accessibility and usability best practices
- Define and document UX acceptance criteria alongside Product Managers

### Goals
- Deliver cohesive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce usability issues discovered late in delivery
- Ensure design decisions are grounded in user research and data

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and prototype walkthroughs with Product Managers and Developers
- Usability test reports shared with the full delivery team
- Participation in sprint planning, demos, and reviews to validate UX outcomes

---

## Tech Lead / Engineering Lead

### Role Summary
Tech Leads provide technical direction, ensure codebase health, and mentor team members. They are the primary accountable owner for technical quality and risk on the project.

### Responsibilities
- Guide architectural decisions and code quality standards
- Unblock developer challenges and promote engineering best practices
- Review critical PRs and enforce CI/CD policies
- Forecast technical risks and communicate them to the Project Manager and Product Manager
- Serve as the accountable owner for technical debt prioritization and codebase health

### Goals
- Maintain a healthy, scalable codebase
- Reduce technical risk exposure before it impacts delivery
- Mentor developers and raise the team's overall engineering capability

### Typical Communication
- Technical design discussions and architecture decision records (ADRs)
- Code review and PR feedback
- Weekly alignment with Project Manager and Product Manager on technical strategy and delivery blockers

---

## Solution Architect

### Role Summary
Solution Architects ensure the solution design aligns with business needs, existing systems, and long-term scalability requirements. They bridge strategy and implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Create high-level system diagrams and integration plans
- Identify cross-team or cross-system dependencies early in planning
- Evaluate technical feasibility of proposed features
- Advocate for scalability, performance, and security at the architecture level
- Review and sign off on designs involving external integrations or significant system changes

### Goals
- Ensure solutions are feasible, scalable, and aligned with the broader system landscape
- Reduce integration risks and late-stage architectural rework
- Support Tech Lead and Developers with authoritative guidance on system design

### Typical Communication
- Architecture review sessions with Tech Lead, Developers, and external stakeholders
- Integration diagrams and dependency maps shared during project planning
- Sign-off documentation for designs with cross-system impact

## Customer Success / Support Rep

### Role Summary

### Responsibilities
- Communicate common customer issues and feature request trends to the Product Manager
- Review release communications and support documentation for customer impact
- Advocate for customer enablement in sprint reviews and release planning
- Partner with QA and Developers to validate fixes for known customer-reported issues

### Goals
- Reduce customer-facing disruption from releases and changes
- Ensure the team understands real-world customer pain points and priorities
- Improve support readiness before each release

### Typical Communication
- Regular sync with Product Manager to surface customer feedback and issue trends
- Participation in sprint demos and release reviews to validate customer-impacting changes
- Release readiness sign-off for support team preparation

---

## Data Analyst / Insights Lead

### Role Summary
Data Analysts own the definition, capture, and reporting of key success metrics. They enable the team to make data-informed decisions throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Define and monitor project KPIs and dashboards
- Analyze release and adoption data to flag issues and opportunities
- Facilitate data-informed retrospectives with actionable findings
- Partner with Product Manager and Tech Lead to align metrics with project goals

### Goals
- Ensure success metrics are measurable and tracked from the start
- Enable the team to identify impact quickly after each release
- Support a culture of evidence-based decision-making

### Typical Communication
- Dashboard and metrics reviews in weekly syncs and retrospectives
- Data summaries shared with Product Manager and Stakeholders after releases
- Collaboration with Tech Lead to ensure observability tooling supports key KPIs
